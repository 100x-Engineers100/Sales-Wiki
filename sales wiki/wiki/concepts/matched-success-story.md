## Definition

The practice of always matching the success story to the prospect's specific background — never using a generic testimonial. The story should feature someone from the same domain, facing the same challenge, who achieved a specific outcome. The closer the match, the higher the impact.

## Why It Matters (for sales)

Generic success stories create zero identification ("that's not me"). A precisely matched story creates immediate recognition ("that's basically me"). The prospect mentally projects themselves into the story and starts seeing their own possible outcome.

## How to Use It

**Format:** Who they were → What problem they had → What they built → What changed (specific, quantifiable if possible)

Keep it under 90 seconds in delivery. One story per call — don't stack testimonials.

**Match priority:**
1. Exact same domain (media agency founder → UAE music agency story)
2. Same role type (manager/exec → SOTI VP story)
3. Same career stage (early professional → Accenture story)
4. Fallback: closest adjacent domain

## When to Use It (trigger)

Step 6 of the call framework — after establishing rapport and FOMO. Also useful mid-call when the prospect raises a "will this work for me?" type question.
