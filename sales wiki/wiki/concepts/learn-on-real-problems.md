## Definition

A reframe for the "I don't have time" objection. Instead of asking the prospect to carve out extra hours, reframe learning as something that happens ON their existing work — not in addition to it. The cohort is designed so that real client/company problems become the projects.

## Why It Matters (for sales)

"I don't have time" is often not about time — it's about fear of overcommitment. The moment you reframe learning as DOING their existing work better (not extra work on top), the calculus changes completely.

## How to Use It

> "Here's the thing — you don't learn separately from your work. You learn ON your actual work. In the cohort, you bring your real problems. If you're running an agency, your client project becomes your cohort project. You're building something you actually need anyway. The 8-10 hours a week isn't extra — it's woven into what you're already doing."

**The three project tracks (mention this):**
1. Your own real problem/use case — bring it and build it
2. Template problems — if you have no idea what to build
3. Company-sourced problems — real companies give problem statements to the cohort

This is especially powerful for founders, agency owners, senior managers — people who have real ongoing work they could apply AI to.

## When to Use It (trigger)

- "I don't have time"
- "I'm too busy with work / clients / projects"
- "8-10 hours a week sounds like a lot"
- Lead is a working professional, founder, or manager with heavy workload
- After disclosing time commitment (8-10 hrs/week including lectures)

## Examples from Calls

Rohit (media agency founder) raised time concern: "if I'm investing money and time, I need to show up and actually get ROI."
Zahid's response: "The whole point is for you to learn concepts and implement them immediately. Take your actual client problems and build your projects from those."
Result: Rohit said "makes sense" and moved forward.
