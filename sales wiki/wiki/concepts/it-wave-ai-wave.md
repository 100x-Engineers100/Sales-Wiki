## Definition

An analogy that makes AI adoption urgency feel historically inevitable rather than sales pressure. The argument: IT professionals were rare and highly paid in the 2000s. Today everyone is in IT and salaries normalized. AI is the same wave. Early movers get disproportionate advantage. The window is now.

## Why It Matters (for sales)

FOMO created by a rep feels like manipulation. FOMO created by an analogy to something the prospect has lived through feels like wisdom. This framing lands because most Indian working professionals in their 30s have personally witnessed the IT wave — they joined companies where IT was transforming everything. It's not hypothetical for them.

## How to Use It

State it as genuine belief, not a closing tactic. Deliver AFTER rapport is established (step 5 of call framework — never as an opener).

> "Here's what I've seen happen — IT professionals were rare once. Companies were desperate for them. Salaries were exceptional. Today? Everyone is in IT. The wave passed and it equalized. AI is exactly the same pattern. Right now there are very few people who can actually build with AI. In 5 years, companies will require it the same way they require Excel today. The people who are early get the advantage. That window is closing."

**For founders specifically — ask, don't tell:**
> "If you don't adopt AI in your company in the next 12 months, do you fear competitors taking your space?"
Let them say yes themselves. Their words carry more weight than yours.

## When to Use It (trigger)

- Lead is on the fence and not feeling urgency
- Lead says "I'll think about it" or "maybe later"
- Lead is comfortable in current role and not seeing immediate need
- Use AFTER rapport is built — never cold

## Examples from Calls

- Used on Harsh: "5 saal mein sab ko AI seekhna hi padega. IT ki tarah."
- Used on Rohit: "If you don't use AI in 12 months, do you fear competitors?" Rohit said "yes, I definitely feel there will be lag." — he created his own urgency.
