## Definition

Every objection or follow-up scenario can be approached in three modes based on reading the lead's energy: Warm (invites reflection, low pressure), Pressure (forces clarity, removes ambiguity), Straightforward (direct, cuts through noise). The rep picks the mode based on the lead — not the script.

## Why It Matters (for sales)

One-size-fits-all responses miss the mark. A warm lead getting pressure tactics will disengage. A cold lead getting warm approaches will ghost. The three-mode system gives the rep flexibility while keeping them anchored to the core philosophy: make them reflect, not convince.

## How to Use It

**Warm mode:** Use when lead is genuinely considering but needs space to think. Invite reflection, ask what's specifically on their mind. Low pressure, builds trust.

**Pressure mode:** Use when lead is clearly interested but not acting — avoiding clarity. Use respectful directness to remove the ambiguity. "What's the real reason?" type questions.

**Straightforward mode:** Use when lead has been unresponsive or is clearly not a fit. Remove your own attachment. "Should I close this from my side?" forces a binary response.

## Key Scenarios Quick Reference

| Scenario | Warm | Pressure | Straightforward |
|----------|------|----------|-----------------|
| Not responding | "Did you get a chance to think?" | "Let me know so I stop following up" | "Is this a priority for you right now?" |
| "I'm busy" | "What's a better time?" | "Usually busy means not urgent — is that it?" | "Follow up later or drop it?" |
| Price | "What did you want to achieve originally?" | "If it got you 50-100% hike, still too expensive?" | "Price or confidence in your effort?" |
| "Need to think" | "What part specifically?" | "What doubt is holding you back?" | — |
| "Will join later" | "Any commitments in 6 months?" | "Most 'later' people never do — real reason?" | "Timing or priority?" |
| Ghosted | "What changed?" | "Did this lose priority?" | "I'll close from my side" |
| Comparing courses | "What basis — content, outcome, price?" | "What's missing here?" | — |
| "No time" | "How does your week look?" | "If not now, when realistically?" | "Is it time or priority?" |
