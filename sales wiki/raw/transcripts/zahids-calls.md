## Call 1 — Harsh (Business Analyst, Software Consulting, 4+ years exp)

### Lead Profile
- Role: Business Analyst at Kinetic Systems (software consulting & support)
- Experience: 4+ years
- AI intent: General curiosity, not tied to specific professional goal
- Attended: Sunday webinar, only for ~30 mins

### Call Flow & Pattern

**Opening (permission-based)**
> "Want to take two minutes of your time if you are available?"

Never assumes. Always asks permission first. Low pressure from the start.

**Discovery questions asked:**
1. "What do you do exactly? Where do you work?"
2. "Is learning AI to grow in your company, or general interest?"
3. "Where would you like to be in your AI journey in the next 6 months?"

Note: Zahid discovers Harsh wants AI for his current job first, then thinks bigger. He does NOT pitch until he understands this.

**Domain mirroring move:**
When Harsh clarifies his BA role is requirement gathering + implementation (not data analytics), Zahid immediately pivots and references AOS Labs (sister company) solving business analyst problems with AI. Makes it tangible and real.

**The Experience Reframe (used on both calls):**
> "People who have 5-6 years in companies... they have practical problems to solve using AI. A college student has no idea what problems exist in the world. Someone like you who knows what problems exist — when AI knowledge comes along with that, that combination is very beneficial."

This is Zahid's most powerful tool. It flips the "I'm not technical enough" fear into an advantage.

**FOMO — IT Wave vs AI Wave analogy:**
> "5 years mein sab ko AI seekhna hi padega. IT professionals were rare once — now everyone is one. AI race mein early join karo toh zyada benefits milenge."

Used after rapport is built. Not pushy. Stated as Zahid's genuine belief.

**Social proof used:**
- SOTI VP (logistics company): Learned AI, now leads AI verticals, automated customer service, saved revenue
- Accenture engineer: Unexpected promotion within same year because he was the only LLM/agents expert

**How cohort was pitched:**
- 6 months, starting June 20
- 4 modules: Diffusion, Full Stack (no-code track available), LLMs, Agents
- 4-6 projects + portfolio + proof of work
- Live Fri/Sat 6pm, 90-120 min + office hours Tue/Thu
- 8-10 hrs/week INCLUDING lectures
- Community = network = real value

**Authenticity signal used:**
> "We don't raise venture capital. We have one program. We want 200-250 people who actually learn."

**Close:**
Never closes. Always: "I'll have Anup send you the brochure and follow up in 2 days."

## Call 2 — Rohit (Media Agency Founder, 8 years in industry, company in 2nd year)

### Lead Profile
- Role: Founder, media agency (TVCs, DVCs, campaigns, YouTube, IPs)
- Clients: Google, YouTube, OnePlus, Flipkart
- Team: 10-12 fixed + 100+ contractors
- AI status: One person doing R&D, using AI for video editing/content at basic level
- Known Sridev (co-founder) personally from 2020-21 singer-songwriter days

### Call Flow & Pattern

**Discovery questions asked:**
1. "What kind of company do you run?"
2. "Have you started using AI internally? Where do you see it coming in?"
3. "When it comes to AI learning as a founder, is that something you're looking for?"
4. "Do you fear competitors eating your revenue if you don't adopt AI in 12 months?"
5. "Do you think AI adoption has to come bottom-up or top-down?"

**Domain mirroring move (strongest in this call):**
Zahid immediately pulled out YaaS (sister company, YouTube as a Service) with specific examples:
- Sportskeeda: fully automated short-form creation
- Unacademy: licensed a sales guy's face for UPSC videos using AI presenter
- Their own Instagram: 80% automated (B-rolls, presenter, scripts)

This worked because Rohit is IN the video/media world. He immediately related ("we tried something similar").

**Key insight Zahid shared:**
> "AI in videos is not playing to strengths, it's hiding weaknesses. Only once you use it, you'll realize what's possible."

Brilliant reframe — removes the perfectionism barrier.

**FOMO triggered:**
> "In the next 12 months, if you don't use AI, do you fear competitors taking over your space?"
Rohit answered: "Yes, I definitely feel there will be some lag."

Zahid used the founder's own words to build urgency. Never imposed it.

**Success story matched to Rohit's world exactly:**
> "One of our star students was running a marketing + tech services agency. After learning the video/images module, he got a contract with a UAE music production company. Their monthly cost was $100k for 2-3 music videos. He charges them $25-30k/month using AI. That's 4x cost reduction for the client, premium pricing for him."

Perfect story for a media agency founder. Specific, relatable, credible.

**Time objection handled:**
> "The point is for you to learn concepts and implement them immediately. Take your actual client problems and build your projects from those. We have three options: your own problem, our template problems, or company-sourced problems."

Reframed time as overlap, not sacrifice.

**Community/network pitched specifically for founders:**
- Founder track vs employment track
- Meet co-founders, connect to VCs
- CMO of AirAsia alumni
- Harvard Business School grads in cohort
- 30% international (Europe, US)
- Graduation party in Bangalore

**Authenticity signals:**
> "We are not the typical edtech that raises 10-20 million. No VC. One cohort. Co-founders are involved. Siddhant (CTO) wants to give people outcomes."

**Price disclosed clearly:**
1,18,490 (one lakh eighteen thousand). No-cost EMI available. 300 hours GPU credits included.

## Extracted Thinking Patterns

### What makes Zahid's calls work:

1. **Permission before everything** — never assumes they have time or interest
2. **Discovery before pitch** — always understands the person's world first
3. **Domain mirroring** — finds something from THEIR exact world (sister companies, use cases, success stories) and reflects it back immediately
4. **Experience as advantage** — flips "I'm not technical" into "you're actually the perfect candidate because you have real problems"
5. **FOMO without pressure** — states it as genuine belief, not as a closing tactic
6. **Success stories matched exactly** — never uses a generic story. Always picks the one closest to the prospect's background
7. **Time overlap framing** — "don't learn in isolation, learn on your real work"
8. **Authenticity as differentiation** — no VC, one cohort, co-founders teach. This is NOT a big edtech.
9. **Never closes on the call** — always moves to "Anup will follow up"
10. **Curiosity over conviction** — Zahid sounds genuinely curious about the prospect's world. Never sounds like he's running a script.

### What NOT to do (inferred from calls):
- Don't pitch before understanding the person's role and goals
- Don't use generic success stories — match the story to their world
- Don't create pressure around pricing — state it clearly and move to value
- Don't overwhelm with info — Zahid himself says "I know I gave you a lot at once"
- Don't close on call 1 — the follow-up system handles conversion

### The core Zahid philosophy:
> "My goal is not to sell the course. My goal is to make them confront their own reality. Once that happens, the sale becomes natural."
(This is also reflected in the objection playbook: Don't convince → Make them reflect)
