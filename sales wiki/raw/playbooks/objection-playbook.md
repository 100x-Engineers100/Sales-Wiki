# Objection & Follow-Up Playbook — Source Document

> Source: 100xEngineers internal sales document
> Purpose: Follow-up scenarios and objection handling approaches

## Core Principles
- Don't "convince" → Make them reflect
- Don't chase → Create curiosity
- Don't dump info → Ask sharp questions
- Always move from logic → emotion → action
- Goal: Make them confront their reality. The sale becomes natural after that.

---

## SCENARIO 1: Lead Not Responding (Cold After Webinar)
- **Warm:** "Hey [Name], did you get a chance to think about what we discussed in our previous call?"
- **Pressure:** "I've been trying to understand if this is something you actually want to explore or not. Just let me know so I don't keep following up."
- **Straightforward:** "I've been trying to reach out — is this a priority for you right now?"

## SCENARIO 2: "I'm Busy" / Ignoring Calls
- **Warm:** "Totally get it — what is a better time for a quick 5-min chat?"
- **Pressure:** "Usually when someone says busy, it just means it's not urgent — is that the case here?"
- **Straightforward:** "Should I follow up later or drop this for now?"

## SCENARIO 3: Price Concern
- **Warm:** "Fair — just to understand, what exactly did you want to achieve through our cohort when you first thought about it?" (Then direct to ROI)
- **Pressure (struggling / under 4 yrs exp):** "If this actually helps you move into AI or get a 50-100% hike in salary, would the price still feel high?"
- **Straightforward:** "Is the issue price, or confidence in your ability to put in effort for the next 6 months?"

## SCENARIO 4: "Need to Think"
- **Warm:** "Of course — what part are you thinking about specifically? So I can help with relevant information."
- **Pressure:** "Usually 'thinking' means there's some doubt. What's holding you back specifically?"

## SCENARIO 5: "Will Join Later"
- **Warm:** "That's fine — do you have any commitments in the next 6 months that's the reason?"
- **Pressure:** "Most people who say 'later' end up not doing it — what's the real reason?"
- **Straightforward:** "Is this a timing issue or priority issue?"

## SCENARIO 6: Interested but Not Taking Action
- **Warm:** "You seemed quite interested — what's stopping you from moving forward?"
- **Pressure:** "Right now it feels like you want this, but you're not acting on it — what's the gap?"
- **Straightforward:** "If this takes you one step closer to your goals, why are you willingly not committing?"

## SCENARIO 7: Ghosted After Initial Interest
- **Warm:** "Last time we spoke you seemed quite interested — what changed?"
- **Pressure:** "Did something shift on your end, or did this just lose priority?"
- **Straightforward:** "If this is not your priority right now, I can close this from my side."

## SCENARIO 8: Comparing with Other Courses
- **Warm:** "Makes sense — what basis are you comparing on? Content, outcome, or pricing?"
- **Pressure:** "What's missing here that matters most to you right now?"

## SCENARIO 9: "I Don't Have Time"
- **Warm:** "How does your workweek look like?"
- **Pressure:** "If not now, when do you realistically see yourself doing this?"
- **Straightforward:** "Is it really time, or is it not a priority? We have senior folks who handle multiple businesses and find time for this."

---

## Closing Lines (Use Anywhere)
- "Just want to understand your situation better"
- "Be honest with me here"
- "No pressure — clarity is more important"
- "Help me understand your thinking"
- "What's actually going on from your side?"

---

## Final Principle
Your goal is NOT to sell the course.
Your goal is to make them confront their reality.
Once that happens, the sale becomes natural.
